Bacalao

CREDITS TO : Gastronomy by Joy

A taste of Spanish influence arrived next with Bacalao. This savory dish features dry-salted labahita (surgeonfish), a testament to Cavite's historical connection to Spain. The fish is rehydrated and cooked with a vibrant mix of vegetables, adding pops of color and freshness.




Valenciana

CREDITS TO : Gastronomy by Joy

The Valenciana brought a surge of nostalgia, transporting you back to childhood fiestas and your mother's loving kitchen. This vibrant Caviteño dish, a personal favorite, must hold cherished memories.




Pansit Kawit

CREDITS TO : Gastronomy by Joy

Pancit Kawit, or more famously known as Pancit Pusit, stole the show at the Caviteño table. Unlike your typical noodle dish, this one boasts a dramatic inky black color, a signature touch from squid ink.
